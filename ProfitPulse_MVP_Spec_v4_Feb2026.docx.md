# **WHAT THIS APP DOES**

ProfitPulse is an intelligent CEO dashboard designed specifically for service-based business owners who need clear, actionable visibility into their financial health—without the complexity of traditional accounting software or the cost of a full-time CFO. The platform layers on top of existing tools to provide real-time insights, cash flow visibility, and plain-English explanations that help entrepreneurs make confident decisions.

By transforming scattered financial data into a simple, visual dashboard with templated insights, ProfitPulse enables first-generation business owners to finally understand where their money is going, track their financial health, and stay informed about their business performance—all delivered in language they actually understand, not accountant-speak.

Service-based business owners—especially first-generation entrepreneurs who started as skilled tradespeople—have all the accounting software but still don't know how their business is actually performing. # **CORE MVP FEATURES**

*These features are included in the MVP build:*

## **1\. Business Health Assessment**

A diagnostic tool that evaluates where the business currently stands. Users answer simple questions about their operations, and the system provides a health score (1-100 scale) across key dimensions. Uses a transparent, weighted formula (e.g., cash runway × 40% \+ profit margin × 30% \+ receivables health × 30%) so users understand exactly how their score is calculated.

## **2\. Real-Time CEO Dashboard**

The central hub showing at-a-glance business performance: cash on hand, weekly/monthly cash flow, profit margins, and key performance indicators. Visual gauges and traffic-light indicators (green/yellow/red) make it immediately clear whether the business is healthy, needs attention, or has critical issues. Manual data entry for MVP

## **3\. Cash Position Tracking**

Simple cash flow view showing: money coming in, money going out, current cash position, and estimated trajectory based on current burn rate. Historical trends and patterns. 

## **4\. Email Alerts & Thresholds**

Email notifications when cash dips below user-configured thresholds, when unusual spending occurs, or when important financial changes happen. Email-only for MVP—SMS alerts deferred to v1.1 to avoid Twilio complexity, phone verification flows, and compliance requirements.

## **5\. Plain-English AI insights**

AI-powered analysis that explains what the numbers mean in everyday language—no accountant-speak. Instead of "Your EBITDA margin decreased 3.2%", users see: "Your profit dropped this month because labor costs went up. Here's what you can do about it." Every metric includes a clear explanation of what it means and why it matters..

## **6\. Weekly Email Summary**

Simple weekly email with business health score and top 2-3 things to know. Example: "Good morning\! Your ProfitPulse score this week: 82/100. Cash flow looks strong. One alert: Expenses up 15% from last month."

## **7\. Scenario & What-If Calculator ⭐ HERO FEATURE**

The key differentiator identified through customer discovery. When Joyce described this feature to her communities, the response was “we’re in, we’re in, we’re in.” Business owners don’t just want to see their numbers—they want to know what those numbers mean for their decisions. This is the “CEO brain” that Joyce brings to her clients, now automated and scalable.

**Core Scenario Types:**

• Break-Even Calculator: “What sales do I need to cover my expenses?”

• Goal Planning: “If I want to hit $X by year-end, what do my monthly sales need to be?”

• Hiring Readiness: “Can I afford to hire an employee? When will I be able to?”

• Shortfall Recovery: “If I miss my January target, what do I need to adjust for the rest of the year?”

• Cash Runway: “How long can I operate with my current cash if revenue stops?”

**How It Works:** Users select a scenario type, input their variables (target revenue, planned services, pricing), and get instant plain-English results with AI-powered explanations. No formulas, no spreadsheets—just answers.

**Example:** A coach plans 7 VIP days/month at $1,000 \+ 1 retreat/quarter at $5,000. If she only does 6 VIP days in February, the calculator instantly shows: “To stay on track for your annual goal, you’ll need either 1 extra VIP day next month OR 2 additional retreat attendees this quarter.”

# **PAGES TO BUILD**

## **Landing Page (Public)**

Warm, approachable entry point that speaks directly to service-based business owners. Headline: "Finally understand your numbers—without the accounting degree." Emphasizes plain-English insights, affordable alternative to CFO services, and the peace of mind that comes from knowing your business is healthy. Clear "Get Started" CTA.

## **Sign Up / Onboarding**

Simple registration: email, business name, industry type. Immediately welcoming tone: "Let's get you some clarity." No overwhelming forms—just enough to get started.

## **Business Health Assessment**

The first-action experience after signup. A guided questionnaire that assesses the business across key dimensions. Simple questions in plain language: "How much cash do you have in the bank right now?" "What were your sales last month?" Results in a health score with transparent formula breakdown and actionable recommendations.

## **Main Dashboard**

The CEO command center. At-a-glance view of: overall health score, cash position (money coming in, money going out, current balance), this month's profit/loss, cash trajectory with visual chart, and any active alerts. Traffic-light indicators (green/yellow/red) make status immediately clear. Historical trends and patterns visible at a glance. Clean, uncluttered design with warm colors.

## **Alerts & Notifications Center**

Central hub for all alerts and notifications. Configure thresholds for cash alerts, set up email preferences, view alert history. Simple controls for what notifications to receive and how often.

## **Scenario Calculator ⭐ HERO PAGE**

The interactive what-if calculator—the main differentiator that got Joyce’s communities excited. Users select a scenario type (break-even, goal planning, hiring readiness, shortfall recovery, cash runway), input their variables using sliders and simple input fields, and get instant plain-English results powered by AI. Clean, inviting interface that makes financial planning feel accessible. Visual progress indicators, helpful tooltips, and clear call-to-actions. Should feel like having a conversation with a trusted advisor, not using accounting software.

## **Data Entry**

Simple interface for entering or updating financial data. Quick-entry forms for cash balance, revenue, expenses. Clear, minimal design that doesn't overwhelm.

## **Settings & Profile**

Business profile management, notification preferences, alert thresholds, user account settings.

## **Login Page**

Secure, simple authentication. Email/password with password reset. Warm, welcoming design consistent with brand.

# **DESIGN SYSTEM**

## **Color Palette**

**Brand Philosophy:** Professional but warm and inviting—not the typical cold, starchy accounting aesthetic. Colors should feel approachable and human.

| Color | Hex Code | Usage |
| ----- | :---: | ----- |
| **Primary Orange** | \#E65100 | Headers, buttons, primary CTAs |
| **Accent Purple** | \#7B1FA2 | Highlights, secondary actions, accents |
| **Success Green** | \#43A047 | Healthy indicators, positive metrics |
| **Warning Amber** | \#F9A825 | Caution states, attention needed |
| **Error Red** | \#D32F2F | Critical alerts, problems |
| **Background** | \#FFF8F5 | Warm off-white page background |
| **Text** | \#333333 | Body text, labels |

## **Design Principles**

* Warm & Approachable: Not cold or starchy—feels like talking to a trusted advisor

* Plain English: No accounting jargon—every term explained simply

* Visual Clarity: Traffic lights and gauges make health status obvious

* Mobile-First: Business owners are busy—must work great on phones

* Minimal Friction: Get to insights fast—no unnecessary steps

* Typography: Nunito Sans or Inter for modern, friendly readability

# **WHAT NOT TO DO**

* Don't compete with QuickBooks—this layers ON TOP of existing tools

* Don't use accounting jargon—everything must be plain English

* Don't build integrations for MVP—start with manual data entry

* Don't build SMS/Twilio for MVP—start with email only

* Don't build complex forecasting—start with simple linear trajectory

* Don't build profitability segmentation—defer to v1.1

* Don't make the design cold/corporate—keep it warm and approachable

* Don't overwhelm with features—start simple, add complexity later

* Don't forget mobile responsiveness—owners are always on the go

* Don't over-engineer (no Redux, no microservices for MVP)

* Don't skip error handling or loading states

* Don't forget Row Level Security (RLS) policies on InsForge
