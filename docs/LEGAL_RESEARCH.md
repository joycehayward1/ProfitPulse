## 2. Compliance regimes that apply (and what we did about each)

### 2.1 Bermuda PIPA — Personal Information Protection Act 2016

**Why it applies:** Joyce/Fusion 4 Business is a Bermuda-based controller. PIPA applies regardless of where customers live.

**Status:** Fully in force since **January 1, 2025** (after several implementation delays). Privacy Commissioner: Alexander White ([privacy.bm](https://www.privacy.bm/guide-to-pipa)).

**What PIPA requires that we addressed in the Privacy Policy:**
- Clear privacy notice listing purposes of collection, types of info, disclosure circumstances, and individual rights → § 1, 2, 7 of the policy
- Individual rights: access, correction, deletion, portability, opt-out → § 7 (also routed through Settings page in-app)
- Cross-border transfer: PIPA permits transfer if the recipient jurisdiction has "comparable protection" OR if the organization takes "reasonable steps (contractual provisions)" — we name our processors and rely on standard DPAs → § 4, § 10
- Third-party processor obligations: the controller must flow PIPA obligations down to vendors → we mention this in § 5 (Security) and § 6 (Retention)
- Breach notification: PIPA requires notification "without undue delay" — we commit to "promptly" notify

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] Register Fusion 4 Business with the Bermuda Privacy Commissioner if revenue / scope thresholds apply (small businesses often exempt — confirm with attorney)
- [ ] Designate a Privacy Officer (PIPA §27 — can be Joyce herself for a small operation)
- [ ] Maintain a Privacy Management Programme document internally (PIPA §5)

### 2.2 GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation (EU users)

**Why it applies:** As soon as ProfitPulse signs up an EU-resident user, GDPR's extraterritorial reach (Article 3(2)) applies.

**What we addressed:**
- Lawful basis stated for each processing purpose → § 11 (Legal Basis sub-bullet)
- Right to access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, objection → § 11
- **Article 22 — Automated Decision-Making.** This is the biggest GDPR issue for ProfitPulse. The "health score" is a profile-based numerical score generated by AI. The CJEU's SCHUFA judgment (Dec 2023) confirmed credit-style scoring qualifies. We addressed this by:
  - Disclosing in § 3 (AI Processing) that scoring is automated
  - Explaining the logic at a high level (which inputs feed the score)
  - Telling EU users they can request human review of any automated decision (§ 11)
  - Stating the score is **informational and never used to make legal/significant decisions** (e.g., we don't deny service based on it) — this is the key carve-out from Article 22's strict prohibition
- Cross-border transfer mechanism: Bermuda has an **EU adequacy decision** since 2008, meaning EU→Bermuda transfers are permitted without SCCs. We mention this in § 10.
- Breach notification: GDPR requires 72 hours to the supervisory authority → § 5

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] Decide whether to appoint an **EU Representative** under GDPR Article 27. **You probably don't need one** if (a) your processing of EU residents is occasional, (b) doesn't include large-scale processing of special categories, and (c) is low-risk. Financial data of EU SMB owners *might* trip this — get attorney opinion. If you do need one, services like ePrivacy or VeraSafe provide EU rep coverage for ~€1,200/yr.
- [ ] Consider whether a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** is required for the AI scoring (Article 35). Likely yes given automated decision-making + financial data, even if low-risk. An attorney can prepare this in 1–2 days.

### 2.3 UK GDPR — UK Data Protection Act 2018

**Why it applies:** UK customers.

**What we addressed:** Largely mirrors EU GDPR post-Brexit. The Privacy Policy lumps UK + EU together for simplicity (§ 11). UK does NOT have an adequacy decision FROM Bermuda — but Bermuda IS recognized by the UK as adequate. Transfers UK→Bermuda are permitted.

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] If UK customer base grows past ~250 records, consider appointing a UK Representative (separate from EU rep)

### 2.4 CCPA / CPRA — California (and other US states)

**Why it applies:** California residents are likely customers. California Consumer Privacy Act + California Privacy Rights Act (2023 amendment, **regulations effective 2026-01-01**).

**What we addressed:**
- **Sensitive Personal Information.** Financial account info IS SPI under CPRA. The Privacy Policy now explicitly:
  - Identifies financial data as SPI (§ 12)
  - Limits SPI use to providing the requested service ("right to limit" baked in by design — we never use financial data for inferences, advertising, or sale)
  - States we do not sell or share SPI for cross-context behavioral advertising
- "Notice at Collection" disclosed at signup → covered in § 1
- Right to know, delete, correct, opt-out of sale/share, limit SPI use, non-discrimination → § 12
- **"Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link:** Required on homepage IF you actually use SPI beyond providing services. Since ProfitPulse does NOT — financial data is used solely to power the user's own dashboard — **we can omit the link** by relying on the carve-out, but we still state the right exists in § 12.

**Other US state laws we cover by extension** (similar structure to CPRA, slight variations):
- Virginia VCDPA
- Colorado CPA
- Connecticut CTDPA
- Utah UCPA
- Texas TDPSA (effective July 2024)

The Privacy Policy lumps these together in a § 13 "Other US State Privacy Laws" rather than enumerating each. This is industry-standard practice for SaaS at ProfitPulse's scale.

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] If ProfitPulse ever passes **100,000 California consumers** OR **$25M annual revenue**, formal CCPA compliance obligations kick in (privacy rights metrics, annual cybersecurity audit, etc.). Not relevant at MVP scale but worth tracking.

### 2.5 PCI DSS — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard

**Why it applies:** ProfitPulse accepts credit cards (via Authorize.net).

**Our scope: SAQ-A (the smallest, least burdensome).**

This is because Authorize.net hosts the payment form — credit card data **never touches ProfitPulse servers**. Under PCI DSS v4.0, SAQ-A eligibility requires:
1. All payment page elements come directly from the PCI-compliant processor (Authorize.net) → ✓ when using Authorize.net's Accept.js hosted form
2. The merchant retains no electronic cardholder data on premises → ✓
3. The webpage that loads the hosted form is served over HTTPS → ✓ (Vercel enforces)

**What we addressed in the Terms:** § 5 explicitly states ProfitPulse uses a hosted payment processor and never stores cardholder data. This is both a customer-facing reassurance AND a useful evidentiary line if a PCI auditor ever asks.

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] **Complete and sign SAQ-A annually.** It's a self-attestation form, ~30 minutes once a year. Authorize.net's merchant portal usually walks you through it. Required by your merchant processor.
- [ ] Confirm with the developer that the payment integration uses Authorize.net's **hosted Accept.js** form and not direct API submission of card data. If direct API → scope balloons to SAQ-A-EP or SAQ-D and triggers significantly more obligations.

### 2.6 Intuit QuickBooks Developer Terms

**Why it applies:** ProfitPulse integrates with QuickBooks Online via OAuth.

**What Intuit requires:**
- Approved Privacy Policy URL must be entered in Intuit Developer Portal → use `https://myprofitpulse.app/privacy`
- Approved EULA / Terms URL must be entered → use `https://myprofitpulse.app/terms`
- App must provide "all required and appropriate warnings, information, and disclosures" to end users about how QuickBooks data is used
- App must comply with [Intuit Developer Supplemental Terms for QuickBooks](https://developer.intuit.com/app/developer/qbo/docs/legal-agreements/intuit-terms-of-service-for-intuit-developer-services/intuit-data-services-quickbooks)

**What we addressed:**
- Privacy Policy § 4 (Third-Party Services) names QuickBooks/Intuit and explains the data flow (financial data from QBO → ProfitPulse → user's dashboard)
- Privacy Policy § 1 (What We Collect) flags that QuickBooks-sourced data is treated identically to user-uploaded data
- Terms § 4 (Your Data) confirms user retains ownership of QBO-imported data and ProfitPulse uses it solely to power the dashboard

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] In the **Intuit Developer Portal** for the ProfitPulse app, paste the URLs:
  - End-user License Agreement URL: `https://myprofitpulse.app/terms`
  - Privacy Policy URL: `https://myprofitpulse.app/privacy`
- [ ] Confirm OAuth redirect URI matches what the app expects (env var `INTUIT_REDIRECT_URI`)
- [ ] Complete Intuit's app security review if you want to be listed in the QuickBooks App Store

### 2.7 CAN-SPAM (US) and PECR (EU/UK)

**Why it applies:** ProfitPulse sends marketing emails (weekly summary, product updates).

**What we addressed:**
- Every email includes an unsubscribe link
- Notification preferences in Settings allow granular opt-out
- Transactional emails (billing, password reset) clearly separated from marketing
- Sender identity (Fusion 4 Business) is identifiable

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] When sending marketing emails, ensure they have a physical postal address in the footer (CAN-SPAM requirement). Bermuda address is fine.

### 2.8 EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)

**Why it might apply:** ProfitPulse uses AI for scoring. Limited applicability for low-risk consumer apps, but worth tracking.

**Our position:** The AI Act classifies systems by risk tier. ProfitPulse's AI:
- Is NOT used for credit scoring of natural persons in a way that determines access to essential services (which would be "high-risk")
- IS a "limited-risk" AI system because it produces output that influences user decisions — this triggers **transparency obligations** (disclose AI involvement, allow users to understand they're interacting with AI output)
- We satisfy transparency by: explicit AI disclosure in Privacy § 3 and Terms § 6, AI-output disclaimer in every AI-generated card on the dashboard

**Joyce-side action items:**
- [ ] None immediate. Monitor enforcement guidance — full AI Act enforcement begins August 2026.

## 5. Gaps and items an attorney should review

These are the items I either **couldn't** answer definitively or where Joyce's specific situation matters more than industry standard:

1. **Bermuda entity registration.** Should the Terms reference "Fusion 4 Business" by its full registered legal name (e.g., "Fusion 4 Business Ltd." or "Fusion 4 Business LLC")? Get the exact entity name from her Bermuda Registrar filing and use it consistently. Also include the registered Bermuda street address in Privacy § 16 and Terms § 18 — required by GDPR for EU controller identification.

2. **Privacy Officer designation.** PIPA § 27 requires a "privacy officer" be designated. For a small business, this can be Joyce. The Privacy Policy should name them by title (e.g., "Privacy Officer, Fusion 4 Business") with an email. Currently we use `hello@myprofitpulse.app`. Confirm.

3. **DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment).** GDPR Article 35 likely requires this because AI scoring + financial data is moderate-risk. Worth ~2–4 hours of attorney time to draft once.

4. **EU Representative under GDPR Art 27.** Depends on whether ProfitPulse "targets" EU residents (yes, if marketing in Europe) or only incidentally serves them. Attorney call.

5. **Arbitration clause.** Common in US-facing SaaS T&C to force arbitration over class action. Bermuda governing law makes this trickier. We left it as standard court-jurisdiction-in-Bermuda. An attorney may want to add a class-action waiver enforceable across jurisdictions.

6. **Chargeback / collections language.** We added a basic "if your payment fails, we may suspend service" clause. If Joyce wants more aggressive collections rights (e.g., charge a reactivation fee), that needs to be specified.

7. **Force majeure.** Standard but not currently included. Trivial to add — covers pandemics, internet outages, AWS regional failure, etc.

8. **Data Processing Agreement (DPA).** Required by GDPR Article 28 when B2B customers (companies) sign up. Currently ProfitPulse is sole-proprietor-facing so probably not needed yet. When you start signing up incorporated businesses with employees, you'll want a standard DPA on file.

9. **California Privacy Rights metrics.** Not required at current scale but become mandatory at the 250,000+ CA-consumer / 50,000+ SPI-consumer threshold.

10. **Health-data-adjacent risk.** Joyce's audience often includes wellness coaches, therapists, and women's-empowerment practitioners. If any clients' financial data inadvertently reveals **health information** (e.g., revenue from therapy sessions linked to client identities), it could touch HIPAA in the US. Not directly applicable to ProfitPulse but worth flagging — the Terms now say "do not upload third-party PII / PHI without authorization."
